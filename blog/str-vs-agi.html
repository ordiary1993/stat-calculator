STR vs AGI: Which Stat Should You Prioritize?

By Wong Yet Chen | May 2026 | 5 min read

One of the most common questions in RPG character building is whether to focus on Strength (STR) or Agility (AGI). Both stats offer powerful benefits, but they serve completely different playstyles. This guide will help you decide which stat fits your character best.

💪 Strength (STR)

  • Higher physical damage per hit
  • Increased carrying capacity
  • Better against armored enemies
  • Slower attack speed

🏃 Agility (AGI)

  • Faster attack speed
  • Higher dodge/evasion rate
  • Better against multiple enemies
  • Lower damage per hit

When to Choose STR

Strength-based builds excel in situations where each individual hit matters. You should prioritize STR if:

  • You're playing a tank or warrior class
  • You face high-defense enemies regularly
  • You prefer slower, deliberate combat with big numbers
  • Your skills scale primarily with STR

When to Choose AGI

Agility-based builds shine in fast-paced combat and prolonged fights. You should prioritize AGI if:

  • You're playing an assassin, archer, or rogue class
  • You want to deal consistent damage over time
  • You prefer avoiding damage rather than absorbing it
  • Your skills scale with attack speed or critical hits

Hybrid Approaches

Many successful builds use a combination of STR and AGI. For example:

  • 70/30 STR/AGI: High damage with decent attack speed
  • 50/50 STR/AGI: Balanced approach for versatile characters
  • 30/70 STR/AGI: Speed-focused with respectable damage

Conclusion

Neither STR nor AGI is universally "better" - the right choice depends entirely on your class, playstyle, and build goals. Experiment with our calculator to find the balance that works for you.
